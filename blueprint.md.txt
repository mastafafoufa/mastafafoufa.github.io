Blueprint: The Digital Architecture for La paresse de penser
Objective
Establish a premier, bilingual digital hub that positions you not just as a tech professional, but as a leading thinker at the intersection of artificial intelligence and human cognition.

Phase 1: The Bilingual Hub (mastafafoufa.com)
The website must effortlessly handle traffic from the Pacific Northwest tech ecosystem and the French literary market. It needs to feel cohesive, not fragmented.

Goal 1: Seamless Internationalization (i18n)

Implement a frictionless language toggle (FR/EN) in the top navigation.

Ensure the URL structure supports SEO in both languages (e.g., mastafafoufa.com/fr/blog and mastafafoufa.com/en/blog).

Do not rely on auto-translate plugins. The tone of La paresse de penser requires precise, nuanced translation.

Goal 2: The "Scientist-Philosopher" Landing Page

FR Version: Highly tailored to support the FYP Éditions release. Prominent book cover, endorsements, and direct links to French retailers (FNAC, Amazon FR, independent bookstores).

EN Version: Positioned for thought leadership, speaking engagements, and networking. Teases the core concepts of the book as universal ideas relevant to the global tech industry.

Goal 3: The Authority Bio

A single, compelling narrative. Frame the journey from engineering foundations at Télécom Paris to tackling advanced applied science, showing how deep exposure to machine learning naturally led to questioning human cognitive shortcuts.

Phase 2: The Content Engine (Bilingual Blog Strategy)
A static website is a brochure; an active blog is a magnet. By populating the blog now, you build SEO authority and a library of shareable assets ahead of the publication date.

Goal 4: The 6-Week Pre-Launch Pipeline

Draft and schedule three cornerstone essays in both languages. These should be 800–1,200 words each, offering high value without giving away the entire book.

Recommended Initial Blog Topics:

The NLP Connection / La Connexion NLP

English: "What Large Language Models Taught Me About Human Mental Laziness." (Exploring how algorithms predict the next word, much like how humans rely on heuristics).

Français: "Ce que les modèles de langage nous apprennent sur nos propres raccourcis cognitifs."

The Silicon Valley Contrast / Le Contraste Technologique

English: "Designing for Friction: Why We Need Slower Thinking in Fast Tech."

Français: "L'éloge de la friction : Pourquoi la technologie devrait nous forcer à penser plus lentement."

The Core Thesis Teaser / L'Aperçu du Livre

English: "The Laziness of Thinking: An Introduction to My Upcoming Book."

Français: "Pourquoi j'ai écrit La paresse de penser : Une exploration de l'esprit moderne."

Phase 3: Amplification & Network Leverage
Once the foundational content is live, the focus shifts to driving the right eyes to the site.

Goal 5: Strategic Cross-Pollination

Publish excerpts of the English blog posts natively on LinkedIn, driving traffic back to the main site for the full article.

Pitch the French versions of these articles as guest op-eds to French tech publications or business journals (e.g., Usine Nouvelle, Maddyness), linking back to the book's landing page.

Goal 6: Institutional Activation

Prepare a digital press kit (author bio, high-res photos, book synopsis, sample Q&A).

Tap into alumni networks. Offer an exclusive webinar or Q&A session around the book's themes to the alumni associations of your past academic institutions, providing them with a high-value event while securing a warm, established audience for the book launch.
